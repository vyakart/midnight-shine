Explorations at the Edge of Form and Meaning

Brazilian Jiu‑Jitsu, astrophotography, mathematics, physics, effective altruism, phenomenology, meditation, and design—woven into a single practice.

Contact

About

I build a life of inquiry across body, mind, and world. On the mat, under a sky full of signal, at the whiteboard, and in the studio, I iterate. My work blends rigor and intuition: test, observe, refine. Minimal surfaces, bold edges, clear structure—then a single detail that turns the image.

Domains

Brazilian Jiu‑Jitsu

I return to first principles: frames, levers, timing. BJJ is embodied problem‑solving—reducing complexity to posture, connection, and pressure. It’s a moving proof: if an idea survives resistance, it earns the right to stay.

Astrophotography

Night work slows the mind. Tracking faint light across hours teaches patience and calibration. The reward is more than an image; it’s a lived sense of scale—quiet distances, color older than language, chaos that resolves with enough exposure.

Mathematics & Physics

I’m drawn to models that vanish into clarity. The elegance of a proof, the humility of a wrong assumption, the relief of a constraint that unlocks the solution. I look for structures that compress reality without flattening it.

Effective Altruism

I care about outcomes. Evidence matters, as do counterfactuals and tractability. I try to place effort where it compounds—choosing projects by expected value, not social momentum. It’s a practice of attention, honesty, and iteration.

Phenomenology

Before ideas, there is experience. I study how meaning shows up: the feel of attention, the grain of perception, the way a problem “opens.” Phenomenology makes my work more precise and my design more human—it keeps me close to the thing itself.

Meditation

Meditation is maintenance for perception. A lab for noticing bias, reactivity, and the habits that steer choices. Quiet is not escape; it’s performance enhancement for learning, making, and relating.

Art & Design

I prefer minimalism with saturated edges—economy of form, then a strike of color. Design makes thought legible: structure, rhythm, whitespace, contrast. Good design lets content breathe and the user rest.

Practice

  • Reduce to essentials.
  • Test in reality.
  • Keep the channel clear.
  • Align beauty with truth.
  • Choose what compounds.
